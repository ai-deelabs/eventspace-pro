Chapter 04 · The Method

From brief
to build-out.

A four-step process refined over seventeen years and several hundred load-ins. Tight enough to hit deadlines, flexible enough to serve a brand.

Step 01

Discovery & brief

We sit with you, the venue plans and the budget. You leave with a written scope, a floor plan and a fixed quote within five working days.

Step 02

Design & render

3D renders, mood boards and materials specs. Two rounds of revision included. Sign-off triggers fabrication and permit filing.

Step 03

Fabrication & load-in

In-house carpentry, metalwork and AV teams build off-site. Our load-in crew rigs the room while you focus on guests.

Step 04

On-site & teardown

A dedicated event manager stays on-site. After the doors close, our crew handles teardown — venue returned, fully cleared.

Chapter 06 · The Journal

Questions,
before signing.

A short reference of the questions we hear most often during the discovery call. If something is missing, send it through — we'll write back the same day.

How early should we book a booth build?
For exhibitions and festivals, we recommend booking 8–12 weeks ahead. For activations under 30 m², we've turned around full builds in as little as 14 days when the design brief is tight.
Do you handle permits and venue compliance?
Yes. Health permits for food booths, electrical certification, fire safety and venue paperwork are included in our end-to-end production scope.
Can you rent without the full production package?
Absolutely. Our modular booth system is available as a rental-only line — you collect from the warehouse or pay for delivery and self-rig on site.
What's the minimum and maximum project size?
From a single 3×3 m booth to multi-zone pavilions of 800 m²+. We've staffed everything from intimate corporate events to 40,000-attendee festivals.
Do you travel for events outside the city?
We operate regionally with our own fleet, and have produced builds across three countries. Travel logistics are quoted inside the production budget.
